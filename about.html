Jason, TSE Man Fung

Graduate Candidate @ NYU Interactive Telecommunication Program (ITP)

Jason is a graduate candidate NYU Interactive Telecommunication Program (ITP). Prior to NYU, Jason graduated as the valedictorian of the class of 2018 at CityU Hong Kong, then worked a year as a Salesforce engineer at South China Morning Post (SCMP), a leading English-language digital media company led by Silicon Valley veterans based in Hong Kong.

Jason considers himself a hardware product engineer, who has a strong technical background, plus a genuine instinct of design, while he is obsessed with intuitive, functional, yet aesthetic and high-fidelity hardware products, interactions, and interfaces. Always, he gets his hands dirty to invent disruptive hardware with his design, prototyping, and engineering skills. Furthermore, Jason is an evangelist in interdisciplinary studies. He learns uncharted disciplines quickly, as a direct consequence of his interdisciplinary practice, which ended up being advantageous to his making of innovative projects holistically.

Jason is active in the startup landscape. Recently, he consulted for the 93.8M valued blockchain startup, Blockstack PBC, for assessing its Product Market Fit and Developer Experience with rounds of user research studies and presentations. Likewise, He was an engineer and also the lead designer of an award-winning smart home product from MIT Entrepreneurship and Maker Skills Integrator (MEMSI), a startup bootcamp where he teamed up with students from MIT to pull off a hardware product prototype with a sound business plan.

Projects aside, Jason frequently speaks in exhibitions, talks, and seminars to share his perspective on technology and his journey of pursuing passion.
